The Opportunity Is Real. The Process Is Complex.

The Queensland Government spends approximately $35 billion annually on procurement. For capable businesses, the main barrier is process and access, not capability.

The Queensland Procurement Policy 2026 sets aside 30% of that spend ($10.5 billion) for small and medium enterprises. At the federal level, Commonwealth procurement runs to around $100 billion annually, with strong SME targets across major agencies. That shift creates a clearer route for businesses ready to compete for government work.

Frequently Asked Questions

What industries do you work with?

We work with specialist small businesses across manufacturing, industrial services, marine and trade, logistics, and defence-related sectors. If you deliver technical or industrial capability and want government revenue, we can help. Typically, that means businesses with roughly 10 to 100 employees.

Do I need prior government experience?

No. We help capable businesses win their first (or next) government contract. If you have the delivery capability, we bring the procurement and bid expertise, including experience in defence and government procurement.

Is my business ready for government contracts?

We work best with established small businesses that have: (1) Annual revenue of $1M+, (2) Proven delivery capability in their field, (3) Capacity to manage government payment terms (typically 30-60 days), and (4) Willingness to invest time in the tender process. If you meet these criteria and want to grow through government contracts, we can help.

What if I win a contract but can't deliver it?

Part of our assessment process is evaluating your delivery capability. We only pursue opportunities that match your current capacity and systems. If there are gaps, we'll identify them upfront and help you address them before bidding. Our goal is sustainable wins, not one-off contracts that overwhelm your business.

How do you charge?

We offer success-based fee options so you're not carrying all the risk upfront. We also offer fixed-fee project support and retainer arrangements. We'll discuss what fits your situation during your free opportunity assessment.

How long until we see results?

Timeline varies by opportunity type. Queensland state and local government contracts typically move faster (2-4 months from tender to award), while federal opportunities can take 6-12 months. During your free assessment, we'll identify realistic opportunities and expected timelines for your specific business.

Do you actually write the bids and handle submissions?

Yes. We find opportunities, draft bid responses, compile compliance documentation, and manage submissions through AusTender and agency portals. You focus on your business; we handle the procurement paperwork.
